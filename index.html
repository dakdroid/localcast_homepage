Troubleshooting

Device discovery

If your device won't show up in LocalCast:


Make sure that all your devices are on the same network.

Swipe LocalCast away in the multi tasking menu - reopen it

Turn WiFi off and on again on your streaming device.

Reboot all your things (Router, streaming device, receiving device)



Supported devices:


Chromecast & Nexus Player

Amazon Fire TV & Stick

Sonos

1000s of SmartTVs, Media centers and other DLNA receivers

Roku is not supported and AppleTV is not supported on the iOS version at the moment

Video Playback

Videos stutter

Stutter means that your receiver has to stop and buffer the video. Some receivers are smart and will adjust the buffer timings. Some receivers are dumb and will pause every few seconds in order to buffer more.



WiFi

The Chromecast or the Amazon Fire TV Stick both come with a HDMI extender cord and with a extra power supply unit.
USE BOTH!
The extra juice from the power supply can improve the WiFi signal. The extender brings the dongle further away from the TV, which improves WiFi too.



Bottleneck - Internet streams

These are usually web optimized video formats. So the bottleneck is most likely either your internet connection or the streams low download speed. In both cases you can't do much to improve this. Sorry



Bottleneck - Local network streams

Don't try to stream Blue-ray quality movies over WiFi. E.g. the Chromecast only supports 2.4GHz which is not enough to stream those movies. It won't work

Bitrate: Some phones shoot videos with a really high bitrate. See above.

PS3 - as DLNA Renderer

It's not possible. You cannot stream to the PS3. Nope. Buy a Chromecast. Go away now.

Codecs / Formats

All receiving devices support a couple of codecs and most video formats.

For example the Chromecast's supported formats and codecs are listed here.

Most video files are containers which hold a video and a audio file. If only one of both plays, then the other one uses a codec which is not supported by your receiving device. To convert the files on your PC I recommend Handbrake

www.handbrake.fr

Subtitles - Pan - Zoom - Rotation

Only Google Cast devices support these features, e.g. the Chromecast and the Nexus Player


You can find these features on the "Now Playing" screen

Subtitles

If there is a subtitle file with the same name as the video file, you will be asked if the subtitle should be added.

Some DLNA servers support subtitles, some don't. So you might be able to add subtitles from DLNA servers, too.

Opensubtitles.org

LocalCast has a built-in Opensubtitle search. Just start your movie or series and add you sub in a couple of seconds from the net!

Pro Version - Issues

If you are having problems with the in-app purchase:

  1. Uninstall LocalCast
  2. Reboot your device
  3. On a computer, go to the Google Play web portal
  4. Log in with the Google account, which you used to purchase
  5. Trigger the installation of LocalCast to your device from the web portal

If this doesn't help, please email me.
